                                     ABSTRACT

TITLE:    Blockchain-Based Verifiable Credential System For Academic 
          Transcripts With Selective Disclosure (Credify)

DOMAIN:   Blockchain & Cryptography

KEY WORDS: Blockchain, Verifiable Credentials, Selective Disclosure, 
           IPFS, SHA-256 Hashing, RSA-2048, Data Privacy

DESCRIPTION:
The digital transformation of academic administration has created an urgent 
need for secure credential management. Traditional systems rely on manual 
validation and centralized databases, making them slow, expensive, and 
vulnerable to document forgery. To solve these issues, we have developed 
"Credify," a high-performance Verifiable Credential System designed to 
secure the issuance and instant verification of academic transcripts.

Credify uses a decentralized architecture to enable institutions to issue 
digitally signed academic credentials (RSA-2048) over a private blockchain 
ledger. By recording only cryptographic fingerprints (SHA-256) on-chain, 
the system ensures document integrity while minimizing storage overhead. 
A key feature of the project is the "Selective Disclosure" mechanism, 
which allows students to share only specific data—such as GPA or degree 
status—without revealing their entire academic history, significantly 
enhancing user privacy.

The system is built using a professional Python Flask backend and a 
responsive Bootstrap 5.3 frontend. It integrates the InterPlanetary 
File System (IPFS) for secure, off-chain storage and follows W3C 
Verifiable Credentials standards. Credify reduces the credential 
verification process from several weeks to just a few seconds, providing 
a secure, scalable, and user-centric alternative to traditional 
certificate verification methods.

                              CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION

1.1 Introduction
    In recent years, the digital transformation of education systems has increased the demand for secure and reliable management of academic credentials. Educational institutions issue millions of transcripts and degrees annually, which serve as critical tokens for employment and higher education. However, the traditional methods of credential verification remain tethered to manual validation, centralized databases, and physical documents. These approaches are not only slow and expensive but are increasingly vulnerable to sophisticated forgery and tampering.

    [DIAGRAM PROMPT - FIG 1.1: Traditional Transcript Verification Process. Use Mermaid.ai to create a flow diagram showing the current manual process.]

    Academic certificate fraud is a growing global concern. The rise of "degree mills" and digital manipulation tools has made it difficult for employers to distinguish genuine credentials from counterfeits. Currently, verifying a certificate often involves weeks of communication between HR departments and university registrar offices.

1.2 Motivation
    The motivation for Credify arises from the friction inherent in the modern academic lifecycle. Students currently have little control over their own verified data. They must request the university to "prove" their graduation repeatedly. Employers face high costs and long wait times to verify candidates, leading many to skip verification entirely—a risk that compromises organizational integrity.

1.3 Problem Definition
    The current verification landscape is plagued by:
    1. Centralization: Single points of failure leading to data loss or tampering.
    2. Latency: Manual verification cycles that delay hiring.
    3. Forgery: Ease of counterfeiting physical and digital documents.
    4. Privacy Erosion: Lack of selective sharing options for sensitive data.

1.4 Objective Of The Project
    The primary objective is to develop a secure academic credential system that:
    1. Enables institutions to issue tamper-proof, digitally signed transcripts.
    2. Gives students ownership and control over their academic records.
    3. Allows verifiers to validate credentials instantly via blockchain hashes.
    4. Implements selective disclosure for field-level privacy.
    5. Uses IPFS for decentralized off-chain storage.

1.5 Limitations Of The Project
    1. Consensus: Uses a private Proof-of-Authority model suitable for institutional hubs.
    2. Adoption: Success relies on university enrollment in the ecosystem.
    3. Key Management: Security depends on user-end private key safety.

                           CHAPTER 3 — LITERATURE SURVEY

3.1 Introduction
    Current verification relies on a "Three-Party" model (Issuer, Holder, Verifier) but lacks a decentralized trust layer. Prior research into blockchain for education frequently struggled with "On-Chain Bloat" (storing large files on-chain).

3.2 Existing System
    Centralized hubs like National Academic Depositories exist, but they are single points of failure. Older blockchain attempts often recorded full transcripts on-chain, making them unscalable and expensive.

3.3 Disadvantages of Existing System
    1. Privacy: No field-level masking.
    2. Scalability: High costs of on-chain data storage.
    3. Latency: Slow transaction confirmation in public chains.

3.4 Proposed System (Credify)
    Credify introduces a hybrid off-chain model where document blobs are stored in IPFS and only the Content Identifiers (CIDs) and Hashes are recorded on the private ledger.

    The "Issue-Verify" cycle involves institutional signing, IPFS pinning, and ledger mining.
    [DIAGRAM PROMPT - FIG 4.3: Credential Issuance Workflow.]

4.4 Implementation Details

    4.4.1 Authentication & Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
    Implemented using Flask-Login to segregate Administrative (Issuer), Student (Holder), and Public (Verifier) views.

    4.4.2 RSA-2048 Digital Signing & Verification Engine
    The core security layer. Transactions are signed by the Institution's private key and verified by the system's public key to prove authorship.

    4.4.3 IPFS File Storage & Off-chain Content Addressing
    Credentials are stored as JSON blobs on IPFS. This returns a unique CID used for immutable retrieval without bloating the blockchain.

    4.4.4 Block-Hashing & Linked Ledger Persistence
    Every issuance creates a new block containing the `Prev_Hash` of the previous record, ensuring a tamper-evident hash chain.

4.5 Results and Discussion

    4.5.1 Latency Analysis: Manual vs. Automated Verification
    Credify reduces verification time from roughly 14 days (manual) to under 2 seconds (automated).

    4.5.2 Security Analysis: Tamper Detection & Integrity Testing
    Test cases proved that modifying even one character in the stored JSON alters the SHA-256 hash, causing a "Verification Failed" result.

    4.5.3 UI/UX Performance & Institutional Branding
    The use of a Senior-grade UI/UX with smooth transitions and high-fidelity PDF output creates an institutional-ready experience.
